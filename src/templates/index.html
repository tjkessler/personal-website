About Me


I am a doctoral candidate in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of Massachusetts Lowell. My research focuses on machine learning and artificial intelligence, and applications include developing multivariate regression models to predict key combustion properties of potentially cleaner and more efficient fuel compounds.

In addition to having experience with machine learning models such as neural networks, I have significant interest in optimization algorithms such as artificial bee colonies and genetic algorithms for hyper-parameter tuning and input variable selection for neural networks.
